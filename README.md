DriverPass Project Summary
The DriverPass project involved designing a system for a client seeking to improve how students prepare for their driving tests. The client requested an online platform where users could register for packages, schedule driving lessons, take practice exams, and monitor their progress. The system needed to support both customer use and administrative management.
What I Did Well
I clearly documented the business requirements, separating functional from non-functional elements in a way that connected each requirement to a specific user need. My system design diagrams were also well-organized and showed a solid understanding of the interactions between system components.
What I Would Revise
If I revised any part of my work, I would expand the explanations in the system architecture section. While the diagrams were effective, the written descriptions could include more detail about scalability, security considerations, and the reasoning behind certain design choices.
Interpreting User Needs
To understand the user’s needs, I focused on analyzing the client interviews and identifying their main goals and challenges. I translated these insights into system features and requirements that directly addressed the user experience. Considering user needs is essential because a system should solve real problems, be intuitive, and feel meaningful to its users.
My Approach to Software Design
My design process begins with gathering information and constraints, followed by modeling the system through diagrams and component breakdowns. I use strategies such as iterative refinement, user-centered design, and scenario analysis. Going forward, I plan to emphasize risk analysis and validation techniques to strengthen the overall design process.
